Introduction to Data Cleaning

What Is Data Cleaning?

Data cleaning is the second step in the ETL process and is crucial for ensuring the accuracy of insights derived from data analysis.
The 5 steps of the ETL process are: extract, clean, transform, load, and analyze.
Data cleaning involves identifying and correcting anomalies and inconsistencies in data.
Data cleaning aims to enhance data quality making subsequent analyses more reliable and to increase productivity by automating repetitive tasks.

Examples of Anomalies and Inconsistencies

Duplicate Records
Identical or very similar records that appear more than once in the dataset.
Example: Two customer profiles with the same email but different customer IDs.
Missing Values
Instances where information is absent for certain fields within a record.
Example: A dataset of patient records where some entries are missing the age or address.
Inconsistent Formats
Data entries that do not follow a uniform format, making analysis challenging.
Example: Dates entered as DD/MM/YYYY in some records and MM/DD/YYYY in others.
Outliers
Values that deviate significantly from the rest of the data, potentially indicating errors or extraordinary events.
Example: A transaction record showing a purchase amount significantly higher than typical values, which may indicate data entry error or fraud.
Incorrect Values
Entries that contain inaccurate information due to typos, misinterpretation, or misinformation.
Example: A product's price listed as $10000 instead of $100.00 due to a decimal placement error.
